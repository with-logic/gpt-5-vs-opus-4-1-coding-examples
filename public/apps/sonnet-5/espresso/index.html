The Problem With Home Coffee

You've bought the gear. You still don't trust your own shots.

A $4,000 machine and a bag of single-origin Yirgacheffe won't fix dialing-in, channeling, or a roast curve gone flat. Most enthusiasts plateau — same machine, same so-so cup, for years.

01

Equipment regret

You've already sunk thousands into a machine that's two generations behind, with a grinder that doesn't match it — and no easy way to upgrade without losing more money.

02

No real feedback loop

YouTube tutorials can't taste your shot. You're guessing at extraction times and roast curves with no expert watching over your shoulder.

03

Roasting feels gatekept

Home roasting looks like it's reserved for people with a garage and a sample roaster. It isn't — you just need the right machine and someone to show you.

Your Six-Month Arc

From first crack to your own signature blend.

This is why we built the membership around a 6-month term — real mastery has phases, and rushing them just burns beans.

M1
Weeks 1–4

Calibration & first roasts

Equipment installed and tuned to your water, altitude, and kitchen ventilation. You complete your first five roasts and your first ten dialed-in espresso shots, coached live.

M2
Month 2

Reading the roast curve

You learn to read bean temperature, rate-of-rise, and first crack timing — and start adjusting profiles for origin-specific characteristics rather than following a fixed recipe.

M3
Month 3

Extraction mastery

Refractometer-based dialing, ratio experiments, and water chemistry. Most members hit their first "café-quality" shot, by their own coach's judgment, in week 11.

M4
Month 4

Origin exploration

You roast through naturals, washed, and honey-processed lots from three continents, building a real palate vocabulary instead of guessing at tasting notes.

M5
Month 5

Blending & milk-based work

Blend design for balance and consistency, plus latte art and milk-texture coaching if you want café-style drinks at home, not just straight shots.

M6
Month 6

Your signature blend, graded

You finalize a personal house blend and roast profile, cupped and scored by your coach — and decide whether to keep your loaner gear at member pricing or continue the membership.

Questions

Before you reserve your equipment

Do I need roasting or barista experience to join? +

No. Most members start with zero roasting experience and a machine they bought online and never fully understood. Your coach meets you at your level — week one is calibration and fundamentals, not a test you can fail.

What happens to the equipment if I cancel? +

We schedule a free pickup within 5 business days of cancellation. On the 6-month and 12-month terms, you also have the option to buy out your loaner equipment at a member-only discounted rate instead of returning it.

Is roasting coffee at home actually legal and safe in an apartment? +

Yes. Our sample roaster includes a smoke-managed afterburner designed for indoor residential use, and our installation team assesses your kitchen's ventilation during setup. We'll recommend a balcony or patio setup if your unit needs it.

Why a 6-month minimum instead of month-to-month? +

Month-to-month is available, but real roasting skill develops over a curve that doesn't compress — reading a roast curve, dialing extraction, and building a palate each take repetition over months, not weeks. The 6-month term also waives your roaster deposit and unlocks weekly (not bi-weekly) coaching, which is the single biggest driver of how fast members improve.

What areas do you currently service? +

We deliver and install throughout San Francisco, the Peninsula (Palo Alto, Mountain View, Redwood City), and the East Bay (Berkeley, Oakland, Walnut Creek). Marin and South Bay (San Jose) delivery is available with a small logistics fee.

Can my coach be matched to my schedule and taste preferences? +

Yes. During onboarding we match you to one of our Q-graders based on your availability, your espresso vs. filter leanings, and whether you're more interested in origin exploration or technical extraction work.
