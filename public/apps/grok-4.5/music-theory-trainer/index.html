The Chromatic Scale

Western music divides the octave into 12 equal semitones. Natural notes (white keys) are A–G. Sharps (♯) and flats (♭) fill the black keys between them.

C → C♯/D♭ → D → D♯/E♭ → E → F → F♯/G♭ → G → G♯/A♭ → A → A♯/B♭ → B → C

Major Scale

The major scale is the most fundamental scale in Western music. It has a bright, happy quality.

Pattern: W – W – H – W – W – W – H

Chord Builder

Major Triad

A major triad is built from a root, major third, and perfect fifth. It has a happy, stable sound.

Formula: 1 – 3 – 5

Intervals: Root + 4 semitones + 3 semitones
