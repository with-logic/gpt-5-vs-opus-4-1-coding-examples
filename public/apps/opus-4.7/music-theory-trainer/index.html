📖Common Chord Families

Major (C)

Bright and stable. The most fundamental chord in Western music.

R - 3 - 5

Minor (Cm)

Sad, pensive, and introspective. Lower the third by a half step.

R - ♭3 - 5

Dominant 7 (C7)

Tense and unresolved — the driving force of blues and jazz.

R - 3 - 5 - ♭7

Major 7 (Cmaj7)

Dreamy and sophisticated. Adds jazz color to a major chord.

R - 3 - 5 - 7

Diminished (C°)

Dark and dissonant. Stack two minor thirds for suspense.

R - ♭3 - ♭5

Augmented (C+)

Mysterious and floating — neither major nor minor, symmetrical.

R - 3 - ♯5
