What’s in the box

A year of flavor, season by season

Each box is curated around what is abundant and delicious in our fields. Every season brings a new palette to your kitchen.

Spring

Tender greens, alliums, and the first roots of the year.

Alliums

Typical produce

  • • Asparagus, peas, spinach
  • • Radishes, scallions, lettuce
  • • Baby kale, chard, herbs
  • • Spring garlic, rhubarb

Summer

Vine-ripened tomatoes, peppers, berries, and herbs.

Nightshades

Typical produce

  • • Tomatoes, peppers, eggplant
  • • Cucumbers, zucchini, corn
  • • Peaches, berries, melon
  • • Basil, dill, summer squash

Fall

Hearty roots, squash, brassicas, and orchard fruit.

Brassicas

Typical produce

  • • Butternut, pumpkins, carrots
  • • Brussels sprouts, cabbage, kale
  • • Apples, pears, figs
  • • Beets, turnips, potatoes

Winter

Storage crops, hardy greens, and preserved pantry items.

Alliums

Typical produce

  • • Potatoes, onions, winter squash
  • • Garlic, shallots, carrots
  • • Kale, spinach, collards
  • • Dried beans, canned tomatoes

Questions

Good food, straight answers

If you do not find what you need, reach out and we will get back to you within a day.

Each box contains 7–9 varieties of seasonal vegetables and fruits, enough to feed 2–4 people for a week. You will also receive a recipe card, storage tips, and a note from the farm. Add-ons like eggs, flowers, or bread can be included at checkout.

Yes. Life happens. You can pause, skip, or cancel your membership at any time before your next delivery cutoff. There are no long-term contracts or cancellation fees.

We label seasonal boxes with common allergen families such as alliums, nightshades, and brassicas. If you have a severe allergy, please contact us directly and we will do our best to accommodate substitutions or provide a swap list.

We deliver within a 25-mile radius of Willowbrook Farm on Tuesdays and Fridays. We also offer five pickup hubs across the county with evening and weekend hours. You can check your ZIP code in the hero section to confirm service.

We are not certified organic, but we farm without synthetic pesticides or herbicides. We use cover crops, compost, and crop rotation to build healthy soil. We welcome visitors to the farm stand to see how we grow.
