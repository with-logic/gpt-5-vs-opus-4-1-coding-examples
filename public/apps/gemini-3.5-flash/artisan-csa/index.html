The Hands in the Soil

Meet the Farm Stewards

We believe that you should know the faces behind your nourishment. Our team practices careful restorative composting, hand-milling, and flower preservation.

CO-FOUNDER & CHIEF SOIL STEWARD

Farmer Silas & Clover the dog

“ Healthy soil produces happy, crisp spinach. For over thirty years in this valley, we've avoided chemical sprays, planting dense legumes and cover clover to nourish the land naturally. Tasting our spring radishes is tasting absolute regenerative care.

🌾 Favorite crop: Heirloom Asparagus • 🐕 Companion: Golden Border Collie
CHIEF HEARTH BAKERS & ORCHARDISTS

Sarah & Ben Chen

“ Our country sourdough is baked in a custom wood-fired brick masonry oven at 4 AM every single Thursday morning. We stone-grind adjacent heritage wheat grains, ensuring the outer bran fibers remain alive and aromatic.

🌾 Favorite crop: Heritage Stone-ground Rye • 🔥 Oven temp: 510°F Wood-fed
GREENHOUSE & INTEGRATED LANDSCAPE MANAGER

Maya Patel

“ Cultivating 40 varieties of sweet seasonal wildflowers is about more than gorgeous farm table centerpieces—it's about honeybee health. By interplanting marigolds and lavender alongside our brassicas, we foster natural pest controllers and bypass toxic industrial inputs.

🌾 Favorite crop: Heirloom Snap Peas & Asters • 🐝 Apiaries: 8 active honeybee colonies
Answers & Inquiries

Common Questions

How do vacation holds work? Can I select specific crops? Click any query card below to examine our detailed commitments.

Easily request a harvest pause or hold directly through your private digital pantry portal by Tuesday at noon. We will divert your weekly share to local riverside shelters, or pause your billing completely!

Yes, absolutely! Every Monday morning at 8 AM, we send a forecast of what is being harvested. You can log into your dashboard and swap up to 3 items for cellar alternatives (like extra potatoes, garlic, jars of wild honey, or preserved peaches).

Yes, we are fully USDA-certified organic, but we go beyond basic industrial standards! We employ strictly no-till farming practices, integrate grazing farmstead poultry for natural weed control, and feed our soil exclusively with local, hand-turned compost.

We drop off your items in insulated wool-lined cooler bags at your doorstep between 8 AM and 5 PM every Thursday. No need to be home! We simply ask that you leave out last week's insulated tote bag so we can sanitize and reuse it, maintaining absolute zero-waste.
