Problem and solution

A clearer operating model for every customer moment.

XYZ Corp had the talent and customer relationships. What they lacked was one reliable system for seeing risk, coordinating interventions, and measuring whether those moves worked.

The problem

Renewal signals were scattered across five teams.

  • Customer success managers were assembling weekly health updates by hand.
  • Revenue leaders discovered churn risk after executive relationships had already cooled.
  • Expansion opportunities were buried in support tickets, usage events, and sales notes.
The solution

A unified health model with playbooks tied to account motion.

  • Product usage, support load, billing status, and sentiment moved into one customer record.
  • Automated risk triggers routed the right playbook to the right owner.
  • Leadership dashboards tracked churn prevention and expansion pipeline every week.

Measured impact

Proof that customer visibility compounds.

Within one quarter, XYZ Corp moved from reactive account management to a repeatable customer growth motion.

01

Health scoring became operational.

The team defined a model that weighed adoption, executive engagement, ticket sentiment, renewal timing, and commercial history. Every score had a clear owner and a recommended next action.

02

Playbooks replaced status meetings.

Instead of waiting for weekly review, high-risk accounts triggered specific tasks for success, support, and sales. Managers used meetings to clear blockers, not rediscover the facts.

03

Expansion became a shared motion.

Product-qualified growth signals flowed into account plans, letting reps coordinate upgrade conversations around demonstrated customer value rather than calendar-driven outreach.

Final outcome

XYZ Corp made retention measurable, repeatable, and board-ready.

The customer success organization now runs on a shared view of customer truth. Renewal risks are visible, expansion paths are prioritized, and executives can connect operating discipline to revenue outcomes.
